\section{Introduction}
Before constructing a generalized Content-Based Image Retrieval (CBIR) mathematical pipeline, an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) isolates spatial, categorical, and formatting disparities inside the raw data. Since classical feature algorithms (like HOG or Spatial LBP) rely heavily on geometric pixel orientations, understanding inherent structural variance prevents catastrophic model failures.

\section{Dataset 1: Paris Buildings (6k)}
The Paris dataset features 6,412 unstructured images spanning 11 unique architectural landmark categories (e.g., Eiffel Tower, Louvre, Notre Dame).

\subsection{Categorical Distribution}
A core finding of the Paris dataset is its severe natively occurring \textbf{Class Imbalance}. Certain landmarks (such as the Eiffel Tower) possess massively disproportionate image-counts compared to niche locations. This natively bounds global indexing, heavily favoring the over-represented classes during K-Nearest Neighbor searches if distance ties occur.

\subsection{Spatial Resolution & Aspect Ratios}
Unlike controlled laboratory datasets, the Paris architecture set fundamentally varies across extremely disconnected resolutions. 
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Vertical vs Horizontal (Aspect Skews):} Because buildings dictate portrait aspect-ratios, and panoramas dictate landscape, the matrix shapes vary wildly. This necessitated the strict uniform $128 \times 128$ and $256 \times 256$ interpolating resizes implemented in our \texttt{src/features.py} extractor to forcibly standardize sliding-window geometries.
    \item \textbf{High-Variance Noise:} Architectures are severely burdened by extreme spatial noise: sky weather variance, tourists passing the camera, and severe lens perspective distortions (looking up at the Eiffel tower vs looking downward from it).
\end{itemize}

\section{Dataset 2: Food-101}
Food-101 acts as the ultimate stress test for image retrieval due to the biological variance inherent in food.

\subsection{Dataset Scale and Absolute Balance}
In extreme contrast to Paris, Food-101 boasts absolute categorical perfection. The set contains exactly 101 discrete classes, and natively isolates exactly 1,000 unique images per class, netting an enormous dataset size of $101,000$ files. This uniform balance allowed us to securely extract $limit\_per\_class=200$ dynamically to shrink computational overhead massively without introducing algorithmic bias into the search space.

\subsection{Geometric Collapse}
Through visual exploration, an absolute lack of solid form was recorded:
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Absence of Edges:} Food entirely lacks sharp structural delineations (e.g., a mound of mashed potatoes has zero distinct geometry compared to a rigidly built cathedral). This perfectly mathematically explains why HOG (scoring just 1.97\%) essentially flatlined in Part A.
    \item \textbf{Plate/Table Domination:} Over 60\% of horizontal pixels inside average samples map to the plate, the serving table, or the silverware rather than the biological composition. This natively justified the inception of the Bounding Box feature in Part B to intercept these redundant pixels mathematically before they pollute global distribution histograms.
\end{itemize}
